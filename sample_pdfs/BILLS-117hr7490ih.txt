                                 A BILL


 
 To direct the President to submit to Congress a report on benefits to 
 the Russian Federation as a result of an agreement with Iran relating 
                    to the nuclear program of Iran.

    Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the 
United States of America in Congress assembled,

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

    This Act may be cited as the ``Investigating Russian Ambitions in 
Nuclear Negotiations Act'' or the ``IRAN Negotiations Act''.

SEC. 2. REPORT ON BENEFITS TO THE RUSSIAN FEDERATION AS A RESULT OF AN 
              AGREEMENT WITH IRAN RELATING TO THE NUCLEAR PROGRAM OF 
              IRAN.

    (a) In General.--Not later than 5 calendar days after reaching an 
agreement with Iran relating to the nuclear program of Iran, the 
President shall submit to the Committee on Foreign Affairs of the House 
of Representatives and the Committee on Foreign Relations of the Senate 
a report describing the benefits that will accrue to the Russian 
Federation as a result of such agreement.
    (b) Elements.--The report required by subsection (a) shall include 
the following:
            (1) A description of the role Russian negotiators played in 
        developing and negotiating the agreement.
            (2) A description of the expected economic benefits likely 
        to accrue to the Russian economy as a result of the agreement.
            (3) A description of the expected economic and technical 
        benefits likely to be experienced by the Russian defense and 
        nuclear energy sector, including known, planned contracts 
        between Russian and Iranian entities, as a result of 
        cooperation made possible by the agreement.
            (4) An analysis of new avenues for sanctions evasion made 
        available to Russia as a result of the agreement.
    (c) Form.--The report required by subsection (a) shall be submitted 
in unclassified form, but may contain a classified annex if necessary.
